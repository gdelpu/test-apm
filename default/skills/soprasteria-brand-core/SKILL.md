# Skill: Sopra Steria Brand Core

## Purpose
This skill contains the reusable core rules of the Sopra Steria visual identity. Use it as the foundation for every audit or refactor.

## Source of truth
Base decisions on the official brand guideline and official templates/assets when available.

## Reusable core rules

### 1) Logo
- Use official source logo files only; never recreate the logo from scratch.
- The classic logo uses red and orange for the swirl and black for the brand name.
- Monochrome black or white versions may be used when production constraints require it.
- Ideally place the logo on solid white; the original version may also be used on solid light backgrounds or uncluttered visuals.
- On dark backgrounds, use the white version; on solid black, the version with color swirl may also be used.
- On complex visuals, the white logo may use a black `#000000` or purple `#2A1449` drop shadow at 75% opacity, 1 mm distance, minimum size 2 mm.
- Maintain the protection area around the logo and respect minimum sizes.
- Never move, resize independently, recolor, distort, skew, or otherwise alter the swirl or wordmark.
- In running text, write the company name as `Sopra Steria` with capital S's.

### 2) Signature / tagline
- Use the official signature assets only.
- Signature font for the logo lockup is Hurme Geometric Sans 3.
- In standard materials, the signature sits to the left of the logo.
- In social content, the signature is placed at the end of the carousel.
- Do not rewrite, restyle, or manually rebuild the signature.

### 3) Primary color system
Prioritize the primary palette before touching the secondary one.

Primary colors:
- Dark purple: `#4D1D82`
- Light purple: `#8B1D82`
- Red: `#CF022B`
- Orange: `#EF7D00`
- Off-black: `#1D1D1B`
- Grey: `#A8A8A7`
- Light grey: `#EDEDED`
- White: `#FFFFFF`

Rules:
- Primary colors should dominate covers and core branded assets.
- The proprietary gradient is a key brand marker and may be used as a fill, background, border, or accent.
- Use the palette with restraint; avoid too many simultaneous colors.
- White should remain heavily present to keep the overall look light and energising.

### 4) Secondary color system
Secondary colors extend the system but do not replace the primary palette.

Secondary colors:
- Dark blue: `#007AC2`
- Light blue: `#32ABD0`
- Dark green / teal: `#00A188`
- Light green: `#95C11F`
- Pink: `#EA5599`
- Yellow: `#F7B90C`

Rules:
- Use secondary colors sparingly.
- Pair secondary colors with at least one main color.
- Secondary colors are for liveliness, emphasis, and selected highlights — not for the dominant brand impression.

### 5) Typography
- Main font: **Hurme Geometric Sans 3**.
- Secondary font: **Hurme Geometric Sans 4** for main headings.
- For office documents and body text in Word / PowerPoint / Excel, use **Tahoma**.
- Prefer left alignment for introductions, chapter openings, highlighted text, and captions.
- Use justified left for long running text.
- Center only for quotations.
- Aim for ~55–65 characters per line for comfortable reading; avoid going beyond ~90.
- Line spacing guidance:
  - print: font size + 2 to 2.5 pt
  - web: font size + 4 to 6 px

### 6) Icons
- Use Sopra Steria icons where available.
- Icons are outline icons in dark purple `#4D1D82`.
- Shapes should be simple and easy to read.
- Optional color patches should use only 20% tones and remain smaller than the icon.
- Patches should be centered or placed upper-right relative to the icon.

### 7) Visual style and composition
Five recurring ingredients are central to branded communication materials:
- logo
- brand signature
- proprietary gradient border
- title in Hurme Geometric Sans 4
- visual and/or gradient

The brand relies on two core markers:
- a flexible composition grid
- a proprietary gradient

Vertical cover rule:
- logo width ≈ one quarter of document width
- title margins left and right: `3X`, where `X` = width of the swirls

Horizontal cover rule:
- logo width ≈ one third of document height
- title margins left and right: `2X`

### 8) Co-branding
- Separate both logos with a gradient effect line in Sopra Steria colors.
- Sopra Steria logo sits left if Sopra Steria is the initiator; right if the partner is the initiator.
- Ensure both logos are visually balanced in size.

### 9) Accessibility
- Ensure sufficient color contrast.
- Do not rely on color alone to convey meaning.
- Contrast target:
  - at least 4.5:1 for normal text
  - at least 3:1 for large text
- On gradients or images, ensure text sits in a safe legibility area.

## How to apply this skill
When making recommendations, always classify each proposed change as one of:
- strict guideline requirement
- strong recommendation
- safe implementation assumption

If an asset or rule is missing, do not improvise the brand. Ask for or reference the official asset.
